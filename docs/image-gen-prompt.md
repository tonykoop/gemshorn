# Image-Gen Prompts

Concept image only; not fabrication authority. Do not include cut-ready dimensions, hole locations, or toolpaths.

## Hero Prompt

A single finished gemshorn, a closed horn-shaped vessel flute, photographed as a real physical object on a clean neutral light-grey studio backdrop. The body is a gently curving cone that tapers from a wide blunt mouth-end down to a sealed pointed tip, the unmistakable ox-horn silhouette of a gemshorn. The wide end carries a fipple/block mouthpiece: a flat-cut beak with a narrow windway leading to a rectangular voicing window and a clean sharp labium edge cut into the upper face of the body. A row of round front fingerholes runs along the upper centerline from just below the window toward the tip, with a single thumb/vent hole implied on the underside near the wide end. Render it as a slip-cast white stoneware/porcelain ceramic body with a soft satin exterior glaze, the windway, labium edge, and tone-hole interiors left matte and unglazed. Realistic scale cues read it as a comfortable hand-held soprano instrument. Soft directional studio light from upper left, gentle falloff and a faint contact shadow, 3/4 product-photography angle, ~50mm lens, high detail, photorealistic, no text labels.

## In-Context Prompt

The same ceramic gemshorn resting on a potter's worn wooden workbench in a quiet ceramics studio, alongside a plaster two-part mold half and a leather-hard greenware blank that hint at the slip-cast workflow. Warm natural window light rakes from the side, dust motes in the air, a shallow depth of field throwing the background shelving and glaze jars into soft bokeh. The instrument is the sharp-focus subject, its curved horn profile and beaked fipple mouthpiece catching the light. Environmental storytelling of a small-batch ceramic instrument shop, concept visualization only.

## Detail Prompt

Macro close-up of the fipple voicing mechanism at the wide end of the ceramic gemshorn: the flat beak mouthpiece, the narrow windway slot, the rectangular voicing window opening into the cavity, and the crisp angled labium edge where the air stream splits. Raking low-angle light grazes across the satin glaze and the matte unglazed window interior to reveal surface texture, the slight chamfer of the window, and the seam where the removable block seats into the wide mouth. Extreme detail on the ceramic surface and the sharp labium, shallow depth of field, no measurements.

## Family-Group Prompt

The full slip-cast gemshorn consort lined up together on a neutral studio surface, arranged largest to smallest: Bass F, Tenor C, Alto F, Soprano C, and Sopranino F, each the same curved closed horn-shaped vessel-flute silhouette with a beaked fipple mouthpiece and front fingerholes, scaling down in graduated steps from a large two-hand bass to a tiny sopranino. A sixth body, the historically informed clay/horn G4 archetype, sits slightly apart with a more rustic horn-like character and fewer holes, distinguishing the authenticity reference from the modern consort. Consistent satin-glazed ceramic finish across the consort, soft even studio light, 3/4 angle, photorealistic, high detail, no text labels.

## Negative / Avoid

No text, logos, watermarks, or fake maker marks; no dimension lines, callouts, tuning numbers, or hole-spacing figures; no open-ended flute or recorder tube (the tip must be a sealed point, not an open bore); no impossible or floating mechanisms; anatomically plausible hands if a player is shown.
